About Logarithms

Logarithms were invented in the 17th century by John Napier as a calculation tool. They simplify complex calculations, particularly multiplication and division, by converting them into addition and subtraction. Today, logarithms have applications in many scientific fields including mathematics, physics, chemistry, and computer science.

  • Decibel Scale: Measuring sound intensity
  • Richter Scale: Measuring earthquake magnitude
  • Computer Science: Analyzing algorithm complexity (Big O notation)
  • Finance: Calculating compound interest and investment growth
  • Biology: Modeling population growth and decay
